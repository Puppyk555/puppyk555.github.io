About Me

I've spent the last year building games in Unreal engine 5, training small machine learning models out of curiosity, and figuring out how to get a room full of people excited about a project. I care a lot about the intersection of creativity and systems — whether that's designing a boss fight or tuning a neural network.

Outside of building things, I lead our school's Computer science Club and am a class representative, where I work with other student leaders and administrators to organize our school's homecoming and prom. I'm currently exploring how reinforcement learning can be used to create smarter, more adaptive game AI.

I want to build games that help struggling patients recovering in rehabilitation, by creating low-stimulation environments that encourage movement and engagement. I also want to explore how machine learning can be used to create more personalized, adaptive gaming experiences.
